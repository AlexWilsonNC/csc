Standard Format

Only cards with the regulation marks of either D, E and F are legal. (Any set or promo released since Sword & Shield)

Player who goes first cannot attack, nor use a Supporter.

English cards only.

Expanded Format

Any set from Black & White (2011) upthrough our recent sets in the Sword & Shield series are legal.

Banned Cards: Click here to see the official list.

Allowed only 1 Ace Spec card per deck, only 1 Prism Star of a given name per deck.

Player who goes first cannot attack nor use a Supporter.

English cards only.

GLC Format

View the Gym Leader Challenge by Andrew Mahone

Any set from Black & White (2011) up through our recent sets in the Sword & Shield series are legal.

Only one of each card with the same name allowed per deck. Except for Basic Energy.

All Pokemon in deck must be of the same type. (ie. only Psychic Pokemon, only Grass Pokemon...)

Banned Cards: Ace Specs, Rule Box Pokemon (ie EXs, Prisms, V's...), Oranguru from Ultra Prism (Resource Managment), Lysandre's Trump Card, & Forest of Giant Plants.

Decks consist of 60 cards, non-see-through sleeves.

Player who goes first cannot attack nor use a Supporter.

English cards only.

2010 Worlds Format

Sets legal: Diamond & Pearl through HGSS Unleashed.

Decks consist of 60 cards, non-see-through sleeves.

You CAN use World Championship decks, cards and proxies for these events, as long as they're printed in color and fit into a sleeve.

Player who goes first can attack, but cannot play ANY Items, Supporters, or Stadium cards.

Players CAN evolve Pokemon on the first turn or first turn they're played with the use of a card's effects. (ie Rare Candy, Spiritomb's Darkness Grace attack...)

When playing a Supporter card, keep in play beside Active Pokemon until end of turn.

Click here for some deck ideas from the 2010 World Championships

2017 Worlds Format

Sets legal: XY - Primal Clash through SM - Burning Shadows

Decks consist of 60 cards, non-see-through sleeves.

You CAN use World Championship decks, cards and proxies for these events, as long as they're printed in color and fit into a sleeve.

Player who goes first cannot attack, but may play Supporters.

Click here for some deck ideas from the 2017 World Championships
